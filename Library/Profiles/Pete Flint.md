# Pete Flint

## Overview

General Partner at NFX. NFX invested in Veras' seed round. Active board member and strategic advisor across numerous board meetings (2023-2025). Was at Seso for 4 years. "Make sure to get Pete's feedback on product because he's a product genius."

## Key Advice

### Strategy & Market

- Get to monetization quickly — $500k-1M in revenue "opens up tons of opportunities and gives you a lot of flexibility"
- Start in the mid-market and work up
- "If you're able to grow revenue to $5M with a single product with less than 5% market share, that's meaningful"
- Having one customer paying 80% of revenue does not demonstrate PMF — need 3-5 customers
- "The best case scenario for SAAS is to become the repository of information. The system of record"
- Replace over integrate
- The defensibility of SAAS is arguably greater than many marketplaces these days
- "Go straight to the biggest opportunity. Don't crawl, walk, run."
- Having multiple products can accelerate the sales cycle — "People take you more seriously"
- Start building adjacencies this year
- Focus on: growth rate, retention, net revenue retention

### Enterprise Sales

- Find the internal champion and build a close personal relationship — make sure they're not too junior
- Put focus on building relationships with key stakeholders — be up there weekly, lunch, etc.
- Enterprise deals driven by making people look good — "Give our users something to make them look good. Short-term critical"
- Give people tools to make them look great to their superiors
- Give easy comparisons against past time periods
- Be highly available during the pilot — help turn negative experiences into positive interactions
- Be willing to send someone on prem and give them the TLC they need
- Drive urgency around the close with onboarding timeline
- "10X ROI becomes a no brainer"
- Make sure we have people dedicated to enterprise relationships — "Make sure they're feeling the love"
- Try to build a predictable sales cycle: "70% of deals will close within 6 months, etc."

### Hiring & Team

- When hiring sales/CS ground level, hire 2 at a time — get 100 resumes, interview the best 10, hire 2
- Creates diversity of perspective, lowers onboarding expense, creates camaraderie
- When bigger, hire "classes" of people (e.g., 6 people starting same date)
- "Great people don't usually get laid off"
- Very important to get quota right — sales people are very extrinsically motivated
- Set very clear expectations and figure out within 3 months if they're the right person
- Have individual quotas add up to more than the target goal
- Hire a good VP/Director for CS — best leverage we have. Want someone who can create process and manage people
- We want someone product minded who can give feedback on the product

### Fundraising & Investors

- Don't worry too much about valuation — $40M is fine, don't push too high to avoid down-rounds
- Cap at $250k per check, maybe 4 partners/investors max
- No discount on product subscription
- "Our customers love our product so much, they want to invest"
- Get other customers to invest as well
- Keep check size small so investors don't think they can dictate roadmap
- Have other customers participate so competitors don't see conflict
- Make sure investors don't have a ROFR
- Reach out to their portfolio companies to see how it is to work with them
- Companies raising nice rounds are break even, growing nicely, and have defensibility (network effects, technology)
- Series A expectation: $2-3M revenue vs $1M in the past
- "Indefinite runway is the wrong way to look at it"
- One benefit of raising is it helps create a moat from potential competitors
- Pete is willing to write a check if needed
- Competition drives good deals

### Execution & Pace

- "Play to win — don't play not to lose"
- "Speed is your #1 asset, so don't compromise speed. Would rather error on the side of too fast and a bit of instability"
- "Speed is better than perfection"
- With large customers, don't move the cheese too much — at some point, do we need to version?

### Product & Engagement

- Drive transparency around whether operators are using the platform as intended
- "Opportunities for saving vs Actualized savings" — are they taking the feedback?
- Gamification: progress bars, streaks, rewards, mascots — make it a game
- Regional level gamification — get them to compete against each other
- HQ level rebates to incentivize portfolio-wide rollout
- Invest heavily in sales/marketing
- Thought leadership: content, blogs, social media, podcasts

### AI

- How can we continue to leverage AI longterm? Get VCs excited
- The VC space is very focused on AI and the power law is very applicable
- Potential AI initiatives: AI Career Coach, AI Learning Coach, AI Skills Assessments
