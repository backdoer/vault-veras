# Jessica Baker (Former Nursa) Conversation

Clinicians are in it for the money

She was laid off by Nursa. Nursa was one of her favorite positions she had and she loved working 

Being a travel nurse was sexy between 2020 and 2022 and then it hit that they needed stability and they started getting full time jobs at facilities. They started having Facilities were stoked about that.

They focused on:
- Clinician appreciation events
- They would put together dinners in areas to meet with clinicians and try to understand the market better
- They cared most about how much they were going to get paid. They had to figure out based on cost of living, competitive rates, etc. how much to pay
- The facilities who paid better had no problem finding temps
- Making sure that the working environment is healthy. Sometimes, the internal teams weren't super kind to the temps because of wage jealousy, etc.
- They would do fun promotions of whoever filled x shifts or the most shifts would get prizes (shoes, dinners, cash, etc.)

They noticed that facilities started using the same temps over and over again because they wanted continuity of care

Help the facilities understand that there's aligned interests and that they should nurture the temp relationships

Nursa merged CS and sales and laid off anyone who couldn't do both

Nursa is following the uber model where they go boots on the ground in a city to try to kick start it

How to get new people:
- Lots of marketing and ads in locations where they wanted to grow. They would try to manage supply/demand.

She loves the idea overall! Resonates.

She primarily worked with the scheduler. They would typically have a small team managing scheduling. She's not super aware of their census reporting or compliance needs or scheduling softwares.

They would pay them on a 1099.
