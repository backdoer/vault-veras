Lisa Young:
- Worked for Quality Dentistry in norcal for 15 years.
- Works as a practice manager.
- You're talking about a lot of moms. Mom calling in sick because of kids. Moms going on maternity.
- Very employee dependent. A dentist HAS to have dental assistants.
- Every office is specific/different and there's a learning curve for the DA/RDH.
- Dental offices will often wonder if it's worth it.
- HAVE to have good subs. Experience subs would be better as they likely know more and can adapt.
- Less differences between offices for hygienists than for assistants. Also likely better at adapting.
- Definitely a field where you could use a temp network.
- 4 different employees scheduled to go out on maternity leave over the next 9 months.
- Even in California, at least one person going out  year on maternal leave.
- Huge effect on the top line when assistants are out.
- They had someone get injured, and they had to shut down a whole op room.
- Production ops and non production (or no charge) ops.
- They try to defer the no-charge patients.
- There are patients you are charging vs patients you don't charge. You don't want to put a bunch of no-charge visits on the same day. Even though you would think it all averages out, it doesn't really. You need to focus on the production clients first.
- Facebook groups? 
- They would use those some. Post covid, hygienists have been really hard to come by. COVID was scary for hygienists - lots of risk. Some people left the field permanently. The schools also closed down for about 2 years, so no new graduates.
- Most hygienists are only working 2-3 days a week. They are often available for other shifts.
- An assistant is usually working 4-5 days a week.
- How do you think about a sub vs a temp? How do you handle that logistically? W/ payroll, taxes, liability, equipment, etc.
- Usually, people would find friends or people they knew to fill in on shifts.
- They would shuffle internally to try to optimize to make the most money.
- She didn't handle payroll directly, so she's not sure how that was handled specifically.
- When people subbed, they were basically like a temp. They would rely on the DA/RDH to find someone to sub. Typically someone they knew. 
- Would it be the employee's responsibilty or the office's responsiblity to find someone? Were they paid more? If an employee is wanting to take some time off for vacation, the office will ask them to go try to find someone. "Try your best." If they can't find anyone, then the office will try to find someone. JOBWISE COULD BE A SOLUTION HERE!
- They would use JobWise more for hygienists than assistants.
- Maternity is a different beast than vacation time off - dependent on state. In CA, you get 4 months off minimum. In Utah, it's much less. A lot of people will just quit in Utah because it doesn't make sense with the little time off. In Utah, they would just replace them because they'd quit. She hasn't seen one come back in Utah yet.
- When someone would go on maternity, they would usually try to find someone or shuffle internally to make it work.
- Is it harder to find assistants or hygienists?
- Utah isn't paying as much as CA. She feels there's a gender wage gap.
- She had a hygnienist getting paid $70/hr in CA and her hygienists are making $35 in Utah.
- The dentists are making a lot more over here and the margins are way higher. The fees aren't very different across CA and UT.
- Licenses vary from state to state. No requirements in Utah for assistants. And assistants can do anything that is not considered "permanent." In CA, you have to have a RDA license.
- They would typically pay the same hourly wage to temps as they did employees if they could, but if someone negotiated, they would comply.
- Dentists typically tend to be really cheap. They don't see a temp as valuable as an employee. A dentist may not see it the same as we see it with the lost revenue every day. "They're not as good as MY assistant and you want me to pay more?? That's not fair to my assistant."
- May have more success in California than Utah.
- A lot of people will take advantage of it and lot of people won't.
