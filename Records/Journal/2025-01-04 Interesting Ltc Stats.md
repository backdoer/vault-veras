# Interesting Ltc Stats

SNF:
- a nursing home is a snf (also considered post acute because they come from acute care at hospital)
- 16500 facilities, projected to go up 3,000 to nearly 20,000 by 2030. 
- 1.2 million residents (projected to nearly double to 2.3 million by 2030) By 2030, all baby boomers (75 million boomers) will be over 65. Life expectancy was 70 in the 1960s. Now it’s near 80. Silver tsunami. 
- Number of people aged over 65 will reach 71 million by 2030. Currently 55 million. 16M new people in that demographic. 
- US SNF market size 179B in 2022, supposed to grow to 232B by 2030. 
- Software market size is $3B by 2030. 
- Average length stay at snf is 28 days 
- 25% of hospitalized Medicare patients transition to snf. Of those residents, 23% of them transfer back to the hospital within 30 days. 
- Revolving door between snf and hospitals
- Medicare will fully cover up to 20 days then partially cover up to 100 days. 
- More than 25% of long-stay snf residents are hospitalized each year. Long stay is anything longer than 100 days. 
- 70-80% of snf residents come from hospital. Other 20-30% come from AL, direct admission, etc. 
- rehab is more associated with snf with snfs offering rehab services. There are also standalone rehab centers. 
- Possible issues leading to snf stay include UTI, pneumonia, Covid, encephalopathy (brain tumor, stroke, etc.), joint replacement surgery, sepsis, pulmonary disease, Parkinson’s, https://www.definitivehc.com/resources/healthcare-insights/top-snf-diagnoses?utm_source=chatgpt.com
- top heavy society. Replacement fertility rate is 2.1 births per woman. Avg US as of 2023 is 1.6. Was  more than double that in the 60s. 
- More adult diapers sold in Japan than baby diapers. US expected to follow suit within the next decade. 
- About 1.4 million individuals employed by SNFs as of 2023.
- Avg bed count and employee count seem to be 100 each per snf. 
- Avg per bed for PCC is about $0.87/bed/day. For 100 bed facility, that’s $32,000/year. TAM is $381M. Expected to double by 2030. 
- Avg PEPM for HRIS is $20. For 100 employee facility, that’s $24,000/year. TAM is 336M. Number of employees is expected to DECREASE slightly (essentially stay the same) by 2030. Because of this, we probably want to price by bed wherever possible. Also wonder if there’s opportunity to empower the existing workforce to be able to handle the upcoming surge in beds. Staff having the power is only going to increase over the next decade. Creates opportunity around recruiting, empowerment, retention, etc. Also tells an interesting story around the need for better software and AI. 
- Over 50% of the market is 10 or more facility rollups.
- Largest players include pacs, ensign, genesis, life care centers of America - each with between 250-300. 
- 85% of snf residents are 65 or older. 

AL:
- 800,000 people in ALF, 30,600 assisted living facilities nationwide 
- memory care is considered a sub-category of assisted living (18% offering memory services)

Acute (hospital):
- Market size is 2.3T and expected to grow to 3.8T by 2033.
- 916,000 staffed hospital beds across 6,120 hospitals in US. This number has declined since the 70s due to shifts towards outpatient care (diagnostics, preventative care, therapies, surgeries, chronic disease management, specialty care, routine health maintenance, urgent care)
- 7.4 million staff working in hospitals. Avg of 1,200 employees per hospital. Avg of 3-5 full time staff per bed. Snfs have avg of 1-2 or less per bed. 
- 56% of hospitals are in chains of 3 or more
- 9 chains own 50 or more hospitals 
- HCA healthcare owns 184 hospitals across 20 states 
- Average of 127 beds per hospital 

Outpatient care:
- $91B market size, expected to grow to $140B by 2030
- $750B market size, expected to grow to $1T by 2030
- 2.5 million staff as of 2023

Home health:
- $150B market size, expected to grow to $250B by 2030
- 6000 home health agencies in US
- 1.7 million people received home health care in 2022 at least one day
- 515,000 employees work in hospice. Avg of 40 employees per business 
- Relatively fragmented compared to snf and hospitals 
- Median stay is 30 days (averages skewed towards long lasting care like dimentia). 
- avg lifetime for someone enrolled in hospice is 92 days
