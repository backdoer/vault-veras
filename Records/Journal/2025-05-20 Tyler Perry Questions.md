# Tyler Perry Questions

Your background's impressive - have heard nothing but great things about you from the team. Listened to your former interview calls. Super impressed. Would love to hear more about how you navigate the sales/product relationship.

I saw you spearheaded a couple products at FileVine - that's awesome. What was your involvement in the discovery process and how did you navigate the early back and forth and building the plane while flying it?
- How do you handle situations where the product doesn’t yet support a feature the prospect is asking for or needing?
- How did your sales/product relationship differ based on whether you were selling the established FileVine product to the established market vs greenfield?

What makes for a successful feedback loop between sales and product in your experience?

Have you ever had to push back on a customer’s request because it wasn’t right for the product or company? How did you handle it?

Can you describe a time when pushback from product or engineering pushback challenged your sales strategy? How did you navigate that?

How do you think about prioritizing which customer feedback or feature requests to push forward internally?

Sounds like you navigated some intense environment and chaos through your career, would love to hear more about that. Some of the challenges and how you weathered them.

I know you mentioned how vital you thought customization was for the FileVine value prop. We've tried to strike the right balance of being opinionated where possible and configurable where needed. How do you think about striking that balance of being subject matter experts vs flexible?

Learnings:
- Started at FileVine when they were at $2M and they're not at about $170M.
- Really tries to keep a unified front with CS and make sure they stay aligned when it comes to customer expectations. Own failures.
- He loves that product would do release notes at FileVine.
- He would keep a comprehensive list of requests/needs from customers then run it up through his leaders. As of recent, FileVine has been starting to get product managers involved in the calls to really hear the customer.
- Would be willing to give discounts as a concession for not having a feature.
- Legal is 5-7 years behind more modern markets.
- 4-6 month implementation period. They have to go all in to get enough value for the cost. He would tell customers this is a heart transplant, not a nose job. They have to do it right the first time.
- Do we need to make high customization ability part of the core value prop? How can we make our product a heart surgery? Or a heart surgery over time?
- He likes to "build equity" in the deal. Be upfront and transparent. Do as much as he can for them through the process.
