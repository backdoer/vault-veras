# 5 Types of Wealth - Sahill Bloom

Life razor
- a razor cuts out the noise/complexity with a simple truth/insight 
   - Occam’s razor 
- a simple statement that synthesizes all else
- Needs to be:
   - controllable
   - rippling in effect
   - tied to identity 
- examples
   - “I never miss family dinner”
   - “I coach my kid’s sports teams”
   - “I never let my friends cry alone”
   - “I attend all my kid’s performances”

Set Anti-goals in addition to goals 
- accomplish x but not at the expense of y or z

“Focus energy on key moments and ignore the rest” - “work smarter, not harder”:
- Lionel Messi (greatest soccer player of all time)
- Warren Buffet (greatest investor of all time)

5 types of wealth:
- time
- social
- physical
- mental
- financial

While you’re focused on maximizing certain areas, keep the dimmer on for the others - don’t turn them off as turning some switches off could prevent you from ever turning them on again. Goals and anti-goals. Prioritize some things, maintain others. These can and will adjust over time. 

You don’t get your kids for 18 years, you get them for 12 - then you become a source of necessities. 

1. Family time is finite - cherish it
2. Children time is precious - be present
3. Friend time is limited - prioritize real friends 
4. Partner time is meaningful - never settle
5. Coworker time is significant - find energy 
6. Alone time is abundant - love yourself 

“No matter how many times you do something, there will come a day when it’s your last time.”

“Enjoy yourself, enjoy yourself - it’s later than you think”

Alice in wonderland is running with the queen but it appears they aren’t getting anywhere. They have to run at that pace just to keep up. Beware this busyness trap. 

Time is worth more than money. But we regularly make decisions that undermine that principle. 

“What is urgent is seldom important and what is important is seldom urgent.” - Dwight D Eisenhour 
1. Manage the important, urgent tasks
2. Spend most of your time on the important, not-urgent tasks
3. Spend less time on un-important tasks 

Write 3-5 things down on an index card every night that you want to get done the next day

Embrace Parkinson’s law - create time constraints to be more productive and efficient. Work like a lion: sprint, rest, repeat. 

Anti-procrastination:
1. Deconstruct large task into smaller tasks 
2. Plans and stakes (rewards/penalties)
3. Action 

Big wins are the result of consistent, small wins.
