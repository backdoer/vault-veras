# Sunroc with Jake Hinkley

Great brand so they're weathering the labor shortage very well. 301 applications that haven't been reviewed yet. 106 open positions. A lot of the applicants aren't top talent or people they want to bring in - these people often already have jobs. Jake is leaning in heavily into recruiting and a proactive approach.

Toughest positions to fill are entry-level positions - lots of turnover. Oftentimes people who didn't expect to end up there - they fell on hard times. High school kids are oftentimes willing to do the work - at that point, Sunroc will train them to move up. They're developing training programs (one in St. George & Cedar City - partnered with the tech schools there) - marketing jobs as trainee jobs to attract talent. Millenials are very career progression focused. 

Entry level labor: $18/hr

CDL training: $14/hr to go to school
After CDL: $20/hr

Conversions and sources:
- Referrals: 34%
  - $1000 employee referral program
- Indeed (they don't like Indeed): 1.5%
- Handshake (a few)
- Jake is head hunting w/ LinkedIn
- Career fairs (doesn't like these - passive)
- High schools (influence next generation to consider construction)

Applicant Pro posts to 50 job boards automatically. Conversion isn't great across the different sources. They're switching to iCIMS by the end of the year. iCIMS is way more sophisticated. Only using iCIMS for the ATS - they'll also use the CRM for recruiting. They won't be using iCIMS for HR. iCIMS is very willing to work with sourcing partners

They are getting hiring managers, recruiters, and sourcers to do all the filtering. Want to leverage technologies. They currently aren't keeping things that up to date - Jake wants to train these individuals to really own this process.

Doesn't like to pay for anything. Currently paying for recruiter subscription w/ LinkedIn & Indeed because he likes to be able to reach out to candidates. Wiling to pay for head hunting capabilites. He would LOVE if JobWise could eventually provide that.

Resonates with him to pay per hire - on a quarterly basis if the employee stays. The more we can align incentives, the better.

He likes the idea of coaching these applicants to present themselves well - give them soft skills.

Entry level is almost more of a commodity.

Heavy Equipment Operator level 3 - at least 10 years with a piece of equipment.
