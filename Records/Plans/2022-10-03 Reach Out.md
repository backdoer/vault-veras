# Reach Out

Hey Paul,

Dude we gotta talk. Look, I now recognize that this came out of left field for you, and for that I’m truly sorry. That was my biggest concern. I went into this under the impression that feedback had been delivered and that you were defensive and reluctant to receive it. This was in no way personal. I honestly believe this is what will be best for the company long term, you included, as you still have equity. 

I do not want this to ruin you. I want to make sure you are taken care of and that you have 1) time to land on your feet, and 2) a meaningful amount of equity that will allow you to see the fruits of your labors when the time comes.

It’s just a dark truth of capitalism that once you raise capital, priority number one becomes returning value to shareholders and no one is exempt from that bar. It becomes an equation of adding value proportional to compensation or being cut. I 100% acknowledge that I could get fired. I’ve never been in this seat at a business before and only time will tell if I’ll be able to adapt. My plan is to do the best that I can for as long as I can. 

Anyways, I realize you’ll likely never forgive me and I understand that. At this point, we can’t change what’s happened. We can only move forward, and I want to make sure you and your family are taken care of, especially financially, as well as I can.
