# Pricing Questions

Is pay pre-defined based on hours in job listing? Or can it be adjusted based on how many hours they actually work? It's seeming like we may need to do it based on how many they actually work.

Paid lunch vs unpaid lunch. Lunch is not paid.

W2 vs 1099. We're going to do W2.

Should experience level factor into wage at all? For both us and employer.

Certain color scrubs?

Pricing Models:
1. Flat fee ($60)
  - Doesn't matter to us how much they pay their hygienist, but it does matter to them
2. Percentage + flat fee (10% of wage to cover FICA + $60)
   - Matters to both of us how much they pay their hygienist
3. Percentage (30%)
   - Matters to both of us how much they pay their hygienist
4. Double percentage (10% of wage to cover FICA + 30%)
5. All-in model ($55/hour to have someone come in)
   - Doesn't matter to them how much they pay their hygienist, but it does matter to us

- Hygienists make more. Percentage makes us more.
- Implications of other industries.
