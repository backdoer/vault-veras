# Counsel from Ben Johnson on Culture at Google

He'd been there longer than 92% of the other engineers

He was there for 10 years and traveled across several different offices.

Psychological safety is the biggest factor of high performing teams.

- Be willing to fail
- Be willing to admit when you fail
- Support each other
- Don't blame
- Emphasize cooperation over competitiveness 
- Peer bonuses
- Ability to do shoutouts, kudos, etc.
- "If it's a money problem, it's an easy problem. If it's a time problem, it's a hard problem."

Bad parts:
- Process and bureaucracy as they grew
- Antagonism between leadership and employees
- They're now having a mass exodus
- They're trying to protect their bottom line (Innovator's Dilemma)

Amazon has 17 leadership principles that are public - he recommends reading these and stealing them.

He recommends less than 10, personally. Make those values a part of you everyday interactions. Find ways to constantly be bringing up the values (hashtags, semantics, naming conventions, etc.)

Trust is huge. If they're not living up to trust, be willing and ready to get rid of them quickly.

Give everyone else the benefit of the doubt.

Within engineering, accountability is more qualitative than quantitative. Peer reviews, calibration, etc.

Creating a bell curve creates competitiveness. Cooperation over competitiveness!

He likes stressing people out in interviews to see how they react/perform under stress.

With take-home tests, you don't get to see people under stress. But you DO get to see the end product and how they write code IRL.

Have multiple people screen candidates as different people will pick up on different things. Add depth to the process - more steps. Date before you marry.

Limbl does "Why we do what we do" meetings where they show people everyone they're helping with the product. And then they do shoutouts. 

The PR process is huge to the engineering culture. The way you communicate during code reviews says a lot about your culture. It's never personal during code reviews!

"Culture leads to winning." Sometimes, it may feel like you're wasting time focusing on culture, but it's well worth the effort! It's okay to spend time on this.

"Software Engineering at Google" - a book he's reading right now.

Enforce the values. And do it immediately. Defend the culture!

Self deprecation is a great part of culture.
