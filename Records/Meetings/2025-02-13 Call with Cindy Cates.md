# Call with Cindy Cates

A patient only needs MDS every 92 days - that's the minimum.

Make sure we're 2-star staffing

MDS/ADL swings depend on the type of facility

When an MDS assessment is opened, they have 14 days to submit and then 14 more days to submit to CMS

A patient is supposed to have an assessment within 14 days of admittance. A medicare patient has to have a 5-day assessment, regardless.

Usage of medicare depends on the state - some states are trying to push their own programs/agendas.

Her recommendation is to try to regress to the mean wherever possible. "We'll use this CMI". 

"Case Mix Index": That's how PBJ works. This is given to each resident. There's a lot of calculations with it and then is averaged out across the facilities.

CMI values are exactly the same across the board for everyone. 

There's really no one that has figured out how to tie CMI values to necessary hours.

Really focus on the star-rating goals and how we can improve.

Cindy is really good at calculating CMI but they would have different calculations from what CMS calculates - for some reason.

How often is the average calculated?

Isolation is the highest CMI value you can get. Lots of care needed. But if you don't re-code them after leaving isolation, then a higher level will be required and can hurt your PBJ. The ability to re-code can help the clinical side. For ups and downs.

Two approaches:
- Focus on PBJ (make sure you're hitting 2 stars)
  - There's an incentive to code them in a way that will minimize requisite staffing levels
- Focus on the needs of the residents today

Typically, every 92 days is the actual carried out MDS. Because that's what is actually required.

The only way to do a code change is through MDS - so usually, they just don't.

MDS takes an entire team (nurse, activities, social services, tons of interviews) anywhere from 4-6 hours to complete. That's why it's not realistic to do MDS more than the absolute minimum required.

The average staffing level required is done every quarter. So if they anticipate lower than expected, then that can hurt their 5-star review big time.

Overstaffing:
Clinical: There's an incentive to over-staff wherever possible because it benefits the patients
Administrators: That's a big cut on the return.

data.cms.gov - has all of the hours to calculate averages, mds, etc.

Can we develop a historical CMS engine to give recommendations

They typically only calculate PBJ at the end of the quarter when they have to report it.

For MDS, the data is not real time enough to handle surges.
