# Board Meeting

ReadyOn:
- EOR vs Embedded
   - https://chat.openai.com/c/0311630c-be71-47d7-a345-2dec734ccf44
- Closed vs Open (Open creates the constraints of shared responsibility, caginess.)
   - Open also creates the expectation that we'll be able to fulfill right now
- How to go about shared responsibility of attracting and vetting and cost (benefits, insurance, taxes, background checks, etc.)
   - Attracting
     - Upload all your contacts
     - Upload all your shifts (it's at cost so no friction)
     - Add everyone who comes in and temps from different sources
     - Post on social media/paid ads/job boards
   - Vetting
     - Interviewing
     - Background checks
     - References (try to automate)
     - License checks


Aeromark
Hundreds of thousands of dollars a year off one brand - 6 locations

Easier for high skill to go to low skill than vice versa
They think big clients have the capability to solve the problem themselves

Start with the closed SAAS and then eventually offer our own staffing agency for the scraps of meat at the bottom.

Closed pros:
- We can eventually offer higher-cost staffing from the open network
- They're in charge of attracting and vetting
- They no longer expect us to just do it for them
- Gets rid of the caginess


Hybrid closed/open:
- Each employer has to choose whether or not to allow someone into their "contact pool." They can look at the resume, reviews, past background checks, etc.
- When an employer uploads someone, they get added as a vetted candidate and their preference gets set to "Only show shifts where I'm an employee."
- Jury is still out for me on embedded payroll vs. EOR
   - We could potentially still automate a lot of headache with embedded payroll. We would just push the burden onto the employer.
