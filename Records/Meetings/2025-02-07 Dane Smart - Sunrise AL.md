# Dane Smart - Sunrise AL

They used to do performance reports where they would actually measure how long it took staff to perform ADLs. But they stopped.

AL doesn't have as many swings as SNF

30/30/30 in SNF - 30 move-ins, 30 move-outs, every 30 days

They would count their hours daily and adjust accordingly. They would follow a tiered approach:
- Ask part time or overtime people to go home
- etc.

Dane's goals @ Sunrise, in order:
1. Get out of agency
2. Stay on budget
   - Overstaffing
   - Time creep 
   - Missed coding (making sure timesheets are coded correctly)
3. Get out of overtime 

They expect the scheduler to enforce hour constraints and have the hard conversations.

They tried uploading budgets weekly into onshift, but they preferred doing the daily hour check-ins and adjusting.

All of their reports are after a month after - and they don't get the value they need.

OnShift is prepping to release a v3.0 soon. Their goal is to simplify. Dane's main concern is losing functionality/reporting. They're potentially removing budgets.

They try to take a team player mindset and communicate constraints to staff wherever possible

OnShift has a shift template view where it shows how many they want and how many they have. They also display hour counts (over/under) on the day. They have a "Minimum HPPD" and a "Target HPPD". Then they display the census on the day. When displaying people to pick up a shift, it shows how many hours that employee is already scheduled for the week. Dane showed a view with the times of day as the y axis.

It looks like they're doing head count budgeting by template. And then it's tracking how many they have of that amount.

OnShift has a call-off flow that sends out messages/invites to available people. They also let clinicians fill out preferences.

The OnShift UX is terrible. But lots of functionality.

OnShift's customer service has gone down significantly in the last 6 years

Dane thinks a scheduler could handle maybe 2-4 buildings, and that will be part of Sunrise's new model. Their main concern with it is not having a scheduler present in the building to have conversations.

OnShift doesn't have any indicator of quality of care.

OnShift doesn't have any PRN functionality around enforcing PRN constraints.

They try to do their best to not put overtime in templates (employment schedules). That's a rule Dane sets for his schedulers.

Dane would LOVE aligning the goals with the schedule in the platform - our vision.

Dane's reports (he spends 20 hours a week to go through these):
- Overtime (make sure it's not in templates)
- Utilization of workers (look for chances to better utilize under-utilized workers)
   - THIS ONE IS COOL
   - Good chance for AI suggestions 
   - Really interesting, fast way to solve agency/overtime on master schedule
- Agency spend
- Budgets/goals compared to actuals

These reports are what Dane teaches and trains on. 

Dany said having an LLM spin up a summary of all the reports for a week would be way cool.

They highly discourage printing

Could be cool to set up an integration where budgets/goals change based on an SFTP report, or from MDS, or from etc.

Dane likes the idea of red being over and yellow being under. He prefers to be under. That's from a financial savings standpoint.

Could be cool to do a calendar view with shift template on the axis. That then shows how many they have vs they need.

Dane LOVES dynamic messaging and the quick interactions.


They compare the following 3 and push to have all 3 match:
- Target (goals)
- Schedule
- Actuals

Dane agreed with Brian Lankanese that there needs to be leadership/culture enforcing it. But the system can help.

Dane liked the role view because it's easier to organize the data and see the gaps.

Quality of care indicators:
- Time and attendance
- Calloffs
- Are they completing their charting in PCC (% completion rate)

Dane said his main concern with AI suggestions is it takes out the scheduler's perspective.

Dane LOVES the ideas of floor rules and staying in compliance

It's funny because we asked Dane if there was anything he would add if he could wave a magic wand, and he didn't know. But we brought up floor rules and he said he LOVED it and has been begging onshift.

Dane said they've been shopping around for a different scheduler for the last 3 years and haven't seen anything that has had what they need until today! 🎉

Dane would love a system that does scheduling and time and attendance well in one system. He REALLY wants a consolidated system.
