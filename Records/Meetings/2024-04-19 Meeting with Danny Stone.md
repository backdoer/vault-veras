# Meeting with Danny Stone

Benefits:
- Cost savings
- "This product is going to take care of your nurses."

Most softwares in the industry have a heavy focus on patients - this is cool in that it focuses on the nurses and their well-being. You want to win over nurses.

Onboarding experiences are vital. Make it smooth and simple. Needs to be perfect.

Response times are everything in support. 24/7 coverage. Could do a BPO outsourcing.

"It's all about your support experience. "

You gotta realize that administration has 100 things on their plate on top of using your software.

He echoed the idea of bringing a scheduler in-house and doing the outsourced service.

Have your onboarder and your CSM be the same person.

Support structures will often fall short in misunderstanding the complexity of the software.

If you want people running support who are less sophisticated, you need to build your product to support that kind of experience (like Netflix surfacing specific error codes for you to give to the support rep who can then understand what that means and handle without eng access).

Ideal hire:
- Get someone who is excited about the product and the vision
- Get someone who can get buy-in from the nurses
- Get someone who is willing to get down and dirty and into the weeds with the facility. Dig deep and build relationships. Hands on.
- Renewal conversations become really easy all of a sudden because the customer sees it as buying the support/success rep.
- Have someone who isn't afraid to go knock on doors and leverage relationships to get their objective done (e.g. reaching out to admins to get the necessary leverage; have the discussion with the scheduler on why they're not adopting)

Ratios:
- You want to make sure you're not compromising your rep's ability to really build the relationships and get into the weeds with the facility.

Start measuring engagement metrics. Time to get back into Tableau and fullstory.

Build the rep compensation structure around user engagement.

Engagement:
- Logins
- Schedule changes/creates
- Data uploads
- Call ins
- Census changes

This could be a playground for picking up extra shifts.

Where pilots most often fail is when the original outcome that drove the pilot gets buried behind other initiatives.
