# Paychex Call

You can create a worker "In Process" under company endpoints

When crossing state lines, we'll need to go into Paychex and do a manual override

In some states, we'll need to do the same for localities (like Pennsylvania and Ohio)

They offer time keeping (the API documentation is in a different place)

Once you have more than 3,000 active, paid workers on a check, it starts creating a degraded experience. They have some customers who start sharding/partitioning. 

They have a relationship with Hire Right for background checks (they got acquired by Equifax a couple weeks ago). They would traditionally offer this as part of their ATS module. 

They handle ACA compliance, and they have offerings (benefits module), though they would recommend one of the brokers they partner with. They also offer 401(k).

We have the ability to set up overtime triggers.

Paychex has MUCH better service than ADP. Dig into the service level of Paychex and ADP. They would give us an implementation project coordinator. US based. After you leave, you get a dedicated account manager.
