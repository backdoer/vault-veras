# Kenn Garff Interview with Joe and Andy

Would pay different amount for experienced tech than entry level apprentice

Would like a higher touch point to really nurture the lead through the funnel

Questions:
1. Are you a good cultural fit? (not a jerk)
2. Do they have the people skills?
3. Do they have the necessary skills?
4. How proficient are they in different areas?

They like the idea of surfacing check-ins to the current employer as an alert of a possible flight risk. This could also drive an argument for payment in perp. 

Kenn Garff likes to build trust and not milk customers. Sometimes, techs aren't a good fit because they're too aggressive and are maybe milking. 

Biggest inputs for employees are location and pay - next are training, tools, tuition reimbursement, environment of the shop (clean, AC, quality of technology), etc.

Loves the idea of high school partnerships
