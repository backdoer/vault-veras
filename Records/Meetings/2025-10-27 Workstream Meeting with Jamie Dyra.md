# Workstream Meeting with Jamie Dyra

Email: jamie.dyra@workstream.com

She's going to intro us to her COO, Karin. 

Workstream's initial product was hiring.

Huge fan of the Check team and CEO (Andrew)

They used all of their white labeled components outside of Workstream's platform just to see if there was interest. 

Small tiger team tackling the opportunity. They had a SME from ADP helping.

Within a year, they had about 19 small customers (all of whom were existing customers.). They only had 1 churn. Their second year, they also focused heavily on expansion withing existing customer base.

Average customer size in 2024 was 75 employees. Hit over 100 deals. They really hit their stride towards the end of 2024.

They tried leading the sales conversations but it didn't work well initially because they didn't know the industry well enough.

Payroll buyers are a different beast than your regular ICP. They're very distrusting. They know their industry really well. They're ruthless. 

Payroll switching process usually happens towards the end of the year. They lost some big deals because they didn't know what they were doing and didn't know what they didn't know. Problems on both the product and implementation side - their product and CS departments were NOT ready. They didn't understand the playbook.

Payroll is complex whether you have 50 workers or 4500. Laws and compliance aren't different.

Blackout period from December 1st to December 31st for EOY - have holidays covered. Someone needs to be involved always. You'd rather be over-prepared than under-prepared. 

They're on track to hit $4M in payroll processing this year. They want to shoot for $10M next year. 

It takes them 3 weeks to 3 months to implement a customer, given their size and previous HR platform.

Advice:
- Only hire implementation/support people with payroll experience
- Payroll integrations have been a vital part of the offering
- Make sure you have a good point of contact with Check
- Their head of engineering is from ADP. Make sure you have someone who understands payroll.
- Their payroll team has become the springboard for all new hires into Workstream. They need to learn the complexity. 
- Jamie is HR with HR certifications
- They have an attorney who helps with all the legalities
- There will ALWAYS be mistakes in payroll. You can't get it mistake free. But you HAVE to be honest and communicative through the process. That's how you win.
- Check does NOT have the notion of multi-EIN companies. You have to roll it yourself. They're working on it though, as requested by Workstream. Check is GREAT at customer support/service.
