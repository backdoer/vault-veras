# Call with Mari

Was the Chief Nursing Officer for Optolum

In the height of COVID, they did a lot of agency work

She took a step back and is now a nurse practitioner and treats patients all day long because that's what she loves doing. She is a full time contractor now.

She was in Southeast Michigan and they were using lots of temporary workers. There was a time when they were doing 30-40% temporary work.

She oversaw high level initiatives like the financials, the ease of the programs, etc.

Employee before overtime was cheaper than agency but once you started getting into overtime, it may be cheaper to go to agency.

They used OnShift which would blast out to all of their employees and temp pool and then if they weren't able to fill, they would put in a placeholder in OnShift then reach out to agencies and then manually put in the worker into the placeholder.

Some of their facilities would do it on paper or in spreadsheets when management wanted all of them to use OnShift.

She loved how you could do templates in OnShift and put in workers' schedule availability and then see where the weak spots and then try to get repeatable temp staff in those places.

Can't remember how much OnShift was charging but it was per bed.

You could plug in your census every day and it would tell you if you were hitting the required numbers or not. You could configure your ratios for different times of day, etc.

She was raving about OnShift. Said she loved their financial module and the recommended employee (based on overtime, availability, etc.) for shifts functionality.

She doesn't like temp workers - but she says that tongue in cheek because she is currently a temp worker. She feels like temp workers usually aren't very invested and they're not assimilated.

She has always felt like float pools were the real opportunity. She worked at Optolus, Mission Point. They already know the policies and systems and procedures. They're more invested. She feels like the main blocker to people creating float pools is the talent shortage - not a tool shortage.

The things she likes her employees familiar with (consistency):
- Policies and procedures
- Software
- Supplies (needles, etc.)
- Fill out documentation
- Stock the closet

She doesn't like temps because they usually aren't consistent.
