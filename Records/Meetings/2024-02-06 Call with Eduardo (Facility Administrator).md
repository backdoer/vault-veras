# Call with Eduardo (Facility Administrator)

They're working on developing their own agency. Going to take them another year to get that up and going.

They're dealing with agencies over web and phone.

They bring in 2 CNAs and 2 RNs a week and an OT once in a while.

What does it mean when he says he's "creating an agency?"

Spends 5-6 hours a day to find temps when there's a need

They fill through agency about 30% of the time. The other 70% is internal.

The director of nursing will usually post online trying to find people.

He handles "cold calling" - which means he calls people to see if anyone is willing to pick up a shift.

He said some of his employees want to turn off 2 AM notifications and don't want to take additional shifts but he doesn't let them.

They run ads on indeed primarily. They have a contract with Indeed to get all sponsored listings. If they post a nurse position for a week, they'll get 10-15 responses. If they post anything unskilled, they get hundreds of applications.

Calls to fill a shift (all at the same time):
- Full time employees
- PRN pool
- Agency

They pay higher than usual rates it sounds like.
RNs: $70-80
LPNs: $60
CNAs: $35-40

Current software stack:
- They use a mass text platform.
- They use Fusion (their agency).
- They use Paylocity to do HR and payroll. All of their CNAs have the app to stay in sync.

Although they have mass texting software and paylocity, he still reaches out manually to apply some pressure.

The schedule is created monthly. They have control over FTE's schedules so they start by assigning all of that out then they try to fill in the gaps with the above process.

Their workers can request time off through Paylocity and then the supervisor can approve/reject that request.

His biggest pain point is that the generations he's working with aren't very tech savvy.

He likes the idea of temps being able to set their availability and then having a more automated scheduling system than their admin having to go in and manually input schedules.
