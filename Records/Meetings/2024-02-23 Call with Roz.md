PPD IS PER HOUSE! Then you send them places based on ratio.

Don't overcomplicate it - most of your staffing people are CENAs

She sharded her schedules based on position

They plan for budgeted capacity/census of their buildings. Based on the maximum bed counts.

Short term care units are going to have a higher velocity of census change

Most buildings are 80% short term, 20% longterm. Some buildings are the opposite.

You won't usually see a swing of 5-10 people within a week.

If a building gets a citation, it can cause census changes because you can't admit new people until you've cleared your brand.

A lot of buildings will follow their pay schedule for handling scheduling.

12s and 8s. Some have 12 hour shifts and some have 8 hour shifts.

Longterm is typically 12 and short term is typically 8.

Longterm and shortterm are typically in different units. That's a common way to split units.

Ratios depend on the census of each unit.

For non-union buildings, they will choose the schedule segments (8s or 12s) are are easier
