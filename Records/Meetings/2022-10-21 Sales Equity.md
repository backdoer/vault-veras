# Sales Equity

Arguments for more equity:
- discovering the sales cycle
- training future reps
- we’re pre-revenue 
- first 10 people 
- they have a risk of not hitting OTE after ramp up because we’re in discovery 
- dilution is nominal 
- boosts morale and sets precedent of generosity

Arguments for less equity:
- they weren’t going to get equity at all originally
- potentially sets bar too high for future hires
- less experienced
- less educational background

Possibilities: 
- 20k shares (.17% & 25,000 or $6,250/year)
- 30k shares (.25% & $37,500 or $9,375/year)
- 40k shares (.33% & $50,000 or $12,500/year)
- 50k shares (.41% & $62,500 or $15,625/year) 
- 60k shares (.50% & $75,000 or $18,750/year)

Big question: 
Are they the right people to solve the crucial problems that need to be solved right now in sales? If so, let’s be generous and get them as bought in as possible. If not, let’s ask ourselves what we need to do about that and make the necessary changes.

Possible concessions: 
- set milestone around hitting quota for x months
- set milestone of cracking the sales cycle

My current vote:
50k shares
