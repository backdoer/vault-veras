# Connor Call (Pacs)

Current process:
Anually, PACS corporate sends out regional reports with a spend down of everything the locations has spent and then they work with their departments. They then analyze how they're doing relative to other locations within the region.

There's also a monthly regional compare.

Departments: nursing, dietary, marketing, central supply, maintenance

Biggest spend departments are nursing, dietary and central supply

Elite 11 metrics are 16 metrics that all of the locations are measured against. They are updated monthly. At the end of the year, they will pick the top 10 and then the most improved. 3 of the metrics are related to scheduling budgeting.

With NHPPD, daily PPD, dietary NHPPD, raw food spend, agency and overtime/double time, they're adjusting month to month.

If they cut NHPPD by .1, they can save $10k monthly.

They will often cut by having people come in late or go home early.

They staff according to what their census numbers are for the day. They staff based on having one admission for the day.

They budget based on hours. Based on their NHPPD.

Census is based on what the occupancy was at 11:59 PM. 

Shifts are AM (7-3), PM (3-11), and NOC (11-7).

They will ask people what they want to do when they need to shorten/lengthen shifts. On a daily basis.

They think about it as a facility as a whole. Jennifer, the scheduler, can also step in as a worker when needed.

They're mostly adjusting within nursing because of mininum NHPPDs. Dietary is way easier to manage because there are only like 5 people.

When the director of nursing is there, she can act as the RN on the floor. They always need at least one on the floor.

He'll send a message over teams asking people who is willing to be flexible with their hours. Or he'll bring it up in standup.

They have some agencies who are cheaper than doing overtime or double time. It's usually cheaper to bring in agency than do a double.

Min PPD is 3.5 total and 2.4 for CNAs.

Most of their nursing staff is on a 4-2 schedule. Their scheduler is moreso doing that.

They already have insights and visibility in their existing dashboards.

Could be cool to mix in the lower paid employees with the higher paid employees.

The furthest out they know about planned admissions is 2-3 days. Planned discharges are known 1-2 weeks in advance.

Loves the suggested invites, especially if it's taking overtime into account.

They hire people for set rigid schedules. Part timers are the same.
