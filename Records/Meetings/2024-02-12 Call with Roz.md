# Call with Roz

OnShift really missed the mark for her.

They didn't allow you to break down Census and PPD by unit - only by "house" or building. 

They use PCC to keep track of residents, discharges, units, etc. Helps them manage census. Short term care requirements are different than long term care requirements.

Different profiles for personas. Schedulers vs. Admins.

Schedulers should see:
- Current week
- Next week
- Current/next pay period
- Urgent items (unfilled shifts)

Admins should see:
- Current PPDs
- Compliance
- Spend/Savings

Have communication/integration with HRIS so you're aware of new hires coming in

Provide a high level view of all the open shifts coming up.




She had a hard time getting float pools to work because the demand is so high right now for workers, that people didn't really have extra bandwidth.




They will require their workers to use their time tracking software or require their agencies (in contract) to have a time tracking solution that will be reviewed by the employer against their schedule.

Main recommendations on differentiating with scheduling:
1. Be able to break down the scheduling, budgeting, census, PPD, by unit
2. Help them find and get insight into the holes at a high level so they can know where they need to hire, get agency, etc.
